# Check Fee — User Journey

This documents the end-to-end journey of checking whether the correct planning application fee has been paid. It involves **three actors**: the **case officer** (in BOPS), the **system** (automatic emails), and the **applicant** (via a secure response link).

The fee check has two main paths: the officer confirms the fee is correct (quick path), or flags the fee as incorrect and sends a fee change request to the applicant. The applicant responds, and the officer confirms the updated payment.

The officer sees the payment information table (fee paid, payment reference, session ID) and a fee calculation breakdown showing how the fee was calculated (application fee, exemptions, reductions, discount, total). They are asked: *"Is the fee valid?"*

- If **Yes** → the officer saves and the task is marked complete immediately. Jump to the final step.
- If **No** → the officer is redirected to the fee change request form (see step 3).

**Where:** Fee change request form

When the officer selects "No", they are redirected to a form where they must explain:
1. **Why the fee is incorrect** — a plain English explanation shown to the applicant
2. **What the applicant needs to do** — clear instructions on how to resolve the issue

The form includes a guidance dropdown with examples of supporting documents for disability fee exemptions (PIP award letters, NHS letters, blue badges, etc.) to help officers use appropriate language.

Once the applicant responds, the officer sees the applicant's response alongside the original request. They can now confirm the total fee paid by entering the updated amount in the currency input field. If the fee was refunded, they enter 0.

Clicking "Mark as valid" marks the fee as correct and completes the task.
