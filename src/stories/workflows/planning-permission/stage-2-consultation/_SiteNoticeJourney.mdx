# Site Notice — User Journey

This documents the end-to-end journey of a site notice, from the case officer's initial decision through to confirmed display with photographic evidence. It involves up to **three actors**: the **case officer** (in BOPS), optionally the **internal team** (via email), and optionally the **applicant** (via email).

**Who:** Case officer in BOPS
**Where:** Consultation task list → Site notice

The case officer opens the site notice task and is asked: *"Does this application require a site notice?"*

- If **No** → the task is marked complete immediately. Journey ends here.
- If **Yes** → the officer must specify how many notices are needed, where they should be displayed, and how to deliver the notice (email or print).

**Who:** Case officer
**Where:** Consultation → Site notice form (expanded)

When "Yes" is selected, the form expands to show three sections:

1. **Quantity** — how many notices to display (defaults to 1)
2. **Location instructions** — optional text describing where the notices should go (e.g. "on the lamppost outside the property")
3. **Delivery method** — the officer chooses one of three options:

**Option A: Email to internal team** — enters the team's email address. The system sends them the PDF link with quantity and location instructions so they can print and display the notice.

<Canvas of={SiteNoticeStories.RequiredEmailToTeam} />

**Option B: Email to applicant** — the system sends the applicant step-by-step instructions on how to print, protect, display, and report back.

<Canvas of={SiteNoticeStories.RequiredEmailToApplicant} />

**Option C: Print** — the officer enters the display date and downloads a PDF to print themselves. No email is sent.

<Canvas of={SiteNoticeStories.RequiredPrintNotice} />

**Who:** Internal team or applicant (outside BOPS)
**Where:** At the application site

The recipient prints the site notice PDF, protects it with a waterproof covering (e.g. a clear plastic bag), and displays it at the specified location(s) near the proposed development. The notice must be visible from public roads and footpaths.

The site notice must remain displayed for **21 days** (or **30 days** if the application is subject to Environmental Impact Assessment).

**Who:** Case officer or internal team
**Where:** Consultation → Confirm site notice

Once the notice has been displayed, the "Confirm site notice" sub-task becomes available. The form shows:
- A status bar confirming the site notice was created (with a link to download the PDF)
- A date field: *"What date was the site notice displayed?"*
- A file upload for evidence photos of the notice in place
