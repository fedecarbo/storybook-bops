# Press Notice — User Journey

This documents the end-to-end journey of a press notice, from the case officer's initial decision through to confirmed publication. It involves **two actors**: the **case officer** (in BOPS) and the **press team** (via email).

**Who:** Case officer in BOPS
**Where:** Consultation task list → Press notice

The case officer opens the press notice task and is asked: *"Does this application require a press notice?"*

- If **No** → the task is marked complete immediately. Journey ends here.
- If **Yes** → the officer must select one or more statutory reasons from a checklist (e.g. Conservation Area, Listed Building, Major Development). If none of the standard reasons apply, they can select "Other" and provide a custom reason.

When the officer saves, two things happen:
1. The press notice is recorded against the application
2. An **email is automatically sent** to the local authority's configured press notice email address

**Who:** System (automatic)
**To:** The local authority's press notice email (e.g. `press.notices@southwark.gov.uk`)

The email includes:
- Application reference number
- Site address
- Full application description
- The statutory reasons selected by the case officer
- A link to the confirmation page in BOPS where the press team can upload evidence

**Who:** Case officer (checking status)
**Where:** Consultation → Confirm press notice

While waiting for the press team to publish the notice and provide evidence, the confirmation page shows:
- The reasons that were selected
- The date the request was sent
- A prompt to upload evidence, with a "Confirm publication" button

The case officer can also manually send a **follow-up email** requesting confirmation if the press team hasn't responded.

**Who:** Press team (or case officer on their behalf)
**Where:** Link from the email → Confirm press notice page

The press team clicks the link in the email, which takes them to a form where they:
1. Enter the **date the notice was published** (must fall within the consultation period)
2. **Upload a photo** of the published press notice as evidence
3. Optionally add a **comment** (e.g. which newspaper, which page)

Once evidence is uploaded, the page shows the completed state:
- The statutory reasons
- Date requested and date published
- The comment left by the press team
- A thumbnail of the uploaded evidence document with a "Published" tag
- Links to **edit publication details** or **add a new press notice response** (if a new notice is needed later)
